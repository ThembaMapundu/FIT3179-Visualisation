Australian Electrical Energy Production and Usage

This visualisation charts the 16 year shift (2008 - 2023) in Australia's electrical energy, contrasting the steady path of usage with the transformation in production from non renewable energy sources to renewable energy sources. The data sourced from Australian Energy Statistics, is recorded at the end of each financial year (July - June), providing a consistent timeline of the power sector's evolution.

New South Wales and Queensland lead as the nation's powerhoused, each generating a total of over 1,000,000 GWh of energy during the period 2008 - 2023. This is largely driven by there extensive coal fired power stations and significant population bases. Victoria follows as a major contributor, while Western Australia's substantial output is closely tied to its energy intensive mining and recource sector. In contrast, smaller states like South Australia, Tasmanian and the Northern Territory operate on a significantly smaller scale, reflectiong there smaller population and different econoomical profiles.

Data is recorded at the end of every financial year

For each state theres a general decrease in production using non renewable energy sources such as coal and this is a result of a shift to renewable energy such as hydro and wind energy.
