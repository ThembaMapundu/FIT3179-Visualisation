Australian Electrical Energy Production

This visualisation charts the 16 year shift (2008 - 2023) in Australia's electrical energy, contrasting the steady path of usage with the transformation in production from non renewable energy sources to renewable energy sources. The data sourced from Australian Energy Statistics, is recorded at the end of each financial year (July - June), providing a consistent timeline of the power sector's evolution. The energy values are measured in GWh which stands for giga-watt hours.

This visualisation shows the overall energy produced in the 16 year period.

New South Wales and Queensland lead as the nation's powerhouses, each generating a total of over 1,000,000 GWh of energy during the period 2008 - 2023. This is largely driven by their extensive coal fired power stations and significant population bases. Victoria follows as a major contributor, while Western Australia's substantial output is closely tied to its energy intensive mining and resource sector. In contrast, smaller states like South Australia, Tasmania and the Northern Territory operate on a significantly smaller scale, reflecting their smaller population and different economical profiles.

Data is recorded at the end of every financial year

Energy sources that produce small values of electrical energy were disregarded

Brown and black coal have historically dominated electricity generation in New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland, but these states are now experiencing a noticeable decline in coal-based energy production. This shift toward renewable energy sources has enabled consistent electricity output while supporting decarbonization efforts. In New South Wales, brown coal generation ended entirely in 2016 with the closure of the Northern Power Station, marking a significant milestone in the state's energy transition.

Renewable vs Non-Renewable

Tasmania leads Australia in renewable energy, operating entirely on renewables since 2020 and producing the most clean power of any state in 2008-09. While Tasmania maintains minimal fossil fuel use, the most notable transition is occurring in mainland states like Victoria, which shows rapid growth in renewable energy adoption.

The Shift to Renewable Energy

Australia has undergone a significant energy transformation since 2008, with renewable generation surging dramatically while fossil fuel use gradually declines. This shift is most evident in states like South Australia, Tasmania and New South Wales, driven by falling technology costs and climate policies. While decarbonization progresses nationwide, the transition pace varies, with some states achieving renewable dominance while others maintain substantial fossil fuel reliance.

Rise in Clean Energy

Bioenergy has been the leading producer of renewable energy from 2008 to 2023, with hydroelectricity as a close second, particularly dominant in Tasmania and New South Wales. This visualisation highlights the ongoing energy transition and projects a continued rise in renewable generation as Australia accelerates toward its clean energy targets.
