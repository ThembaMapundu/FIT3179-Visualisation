Australian Electrical Energy Production and Consumption

This visualisation charts the 16 year shift (2008 - 2023) in Australia's electrical energy, contrasting the steady path of usage with the transformation in production from non renewable energy sources to renewable energy sources. The data sourced from Australian Energy Statistics, is recorded at the end of each financial year (July - June), providing a consistent timeline of the power sector's evolution.

New South Wales and Queensland lead as the nation's powerhouses, each generating a total of over 1,000,000 GWh of energy during the period 2008 - 2023. This is largely driven by their extensive coal fired power stations and significant population bases. Victoria follows as a major contributor, while Western Australia's substantial output is closely tied to its energy intensive mining and resource sector. In contrast, smaller states like South Australia, Tasmania and the Northern Territory operate on a significantly smaller scale, reflecting their smaller population and different economical profiles.

Data is recorded at the end of every financial year

For each state there's a general decrease in production using non renewable energy sources such as coal and this is a result of a shift to renewable energy such as hydro and wind energy. Since the energy produced from renewable is still overall low as compared to Non renewable energy sources, the renewable energy data has been grouped.

Renewable Energy vs Non Renewable Energy

During 2008-2023, Australia's energy landscape shifted significantly as renewable energy production surged across most states while non-renewable generation gradually declined. This transition reflects the country's accelerating move toward cleaner energy sources, though fossil fuels still maintain a substantial presence in the energy mix.

The Shift to Renewable Energy

Australia has undergone a remarkable energy transformation, with renewable energy production surging dramatically across all states and territories from 2008 to 2023. While non-renewable generation has gradually declined in most regions, renewable sources have experienced exponential growth, particularly in states like South Australia, Tasmania, and New South Wales. This shift reflects Australia's accelerating transition toward cleaner energy, driven by falling technology costs, supportive government policies, and growing climate consciousness. The data reveals a clear national trend of decarbonization, though the pace varies significantly between states, with some already achieving renewable dominance while others maintain substantial fossil fuel reliance.
